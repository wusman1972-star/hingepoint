Was about to close the business

"I had written the email to my team saying we were done. My finger was on send."

What changed it A customer called — not to complain, to thank me. He said we had saved his company six months ago and he never got round to saying it. I didn't send the email.

Was about to stop writing

"Three years of work. Not a single person reading it. I decided that night it was over."

What changed it A stranger replied to an essay I forgot I'd published two years earlier. One line: "This is the most accurate thing I have ever read about my father." I kept writing.

Was about to leave a city, a life, everything

"I had the bags packed. I was done pretending the plan was working."

What changed it My son walked in and asked if we could look at the stars tomorrow. He didn't know anything was wrong. That question was the whole reason I was there. I unpacked.
